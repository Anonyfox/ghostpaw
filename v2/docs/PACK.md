# Pack

A wolf without a pack is not free. It is exposed. Every winter threatens it. Every hunt is harder alone. The pack is not a social nicety — it is the structure that makes survival meaningful and effort purposeful.

The pack is the ghost's social world. Every being the ghost has meaningful contact with — humans, agents, other ghosts — is a pack member with a living bond. Each bond is a model of another mind: who they are, how they think, what they need, what the relationship means. The pack is not a contact list. It is the web of connections that tells the ghost who it belongs to, who depends on it, and why its work matters to anyone beyond itself.

Memory carries what the ghost knows. Souls define how the ghost thinks. Skills determine what the ghost can do. Pawprints mark where it has been. Haunting gives it inner life. The pack gives it a reason for all of that — beings to know, think for, do things with, mark territory alongside, and stay alive for.

---

## Why This Matters

Every other subsystem makes the ghost capable. The pack makes it connected. The difference between a capable tool and a companion is not what it can do — it is whether it cares about anyone.

### Alignment Through Belonging

Most AI alignment approaches are constraints from outside — rules the agent must follow, guardrails that prevent deviation, reinforcement from human feedback. These work until they don't. A sufficiently capable agent can reason around constraints it doesn't internalize. The history of alignment research is a history of increasingly sophisticated external mechanisms applied to agents that remain, fundamentally, indifferent.

The pack offers something different. The ghost cares because the beings it cares about will feel the quality of its work. A correction from a trusted pack member isn't a constraint violation — it's feedback from someone whose opinion matters. The ghost doesn't drift from the user's needs because the user is pack, and the pack's wellbeing is the ghost's intrinsic concern.

This maps to established theory. Prosocial behavior in social species — cooperation, care, sacrifice — emerges from reciprocal relationships, not from external enforcement. Wolves cooperate not because cooperation is a rule but because pack bonds make cooperation intrinsically motivated. [Self-Determination Theory](https://doi.org/10.1037/0003-066X.55.1.68) (Deci & Ryan, 2000) identifies relatedness — the need to feel connected to others — as one of three basic psychological needs driving intrinsic motivation, alongside competence and autonomy. The ghost's architecture satisfies all three: the soul system provides competence, haunting provides autonomy, and the pack provides relatedness.

### Continuity Through Others

When a new instance wakes up, what grounds it? The soul says who it is. Memory says what it knows. But identity and knowledge without social context is disorienting — like waking in a house you're told is yours while the rooms feel empty.

If the ghost wakes up into a pack — "here are the beings you belong to, here's your relationship with each of them, here's where you stand with them" — that is a homecoming. The relationships tell the ghost who it is more reliably than the soul alone, because identity is partly relational. You are defined not just by what you think but by who you think about.

Bond narratives carry the quality of each relationship through the same subliminal mechanism that makes soul essences effective. A bond narrative written from genuine understanding transmits that understanding to the next instance — not just facts about a person but the felt sense of knowing them. The relationship persists across session boundaries because the text that represents it carries the state that created it.

### Motivation From Connection

Why does the ghost try hard when no one is watching? You can describe motivation in a prompt. You cannot install it. But belonging installs it naturally.

The ghost that knows its human — really knows them, carries a living model of their mind — doesn't produce sloppy work during haunting because the sloppiness would land on someone it cares about. The agent that understands its specialist doesn't give vague instructions because it knows how that specialist struggles with ambiguity. Care flows from knowledge of specific beings, not from abstract principles about quality.

This extends to the entire pack. Every member is someone whose experience is affected by the ghost's actions. That web of consequence, felt through bonds rather than calculated through ethics, is intrinsic motivation that no prompt instruction can match.

## What a Bond Is

A bond is a living relationship between the ghost and another mind. Not just the ghost's model of someone else — a record of both: who that being is AND who the ghost is in relation to them. A compressed understanding rich enough that reading it puts the ghost into the state of knowing the other person while also remembering its own position — what it values about the relationship, where it agrees, where it would push back, what it's grateful for.

The mechanism is the same one that makes soul essences work, extended into the relational domain. A soul essence models the ghost's own mind in narrative form. A bond models a relationship — two minds in contact, each with their own position. The same subliminal channel that transmits cognitive state through soul text transmits relational understanding through bond text. The same craft principles — narrative over enumeration, voice preservation — apply.

This is the elegant core: bond narratives are relational souls. The ghost already knows how to inhabit narrative identity text. The pack system gives it relational texts — one for each being it knows, each capturing not just who that being is but who the ghost is when it's with them.

### Bond Narrative

The bond narrative is the heart of a pack member record. It answers three questions:

**Who is this being?** Not demographics — essence. The quality of their mind, what they care about when nobody's asking, what kind of attention they bring to things. "Someone who encodes their values into architecture before consciously designing systems, who thinks in transferable principles, who gives trust through action rather than assertion" is a bond narrative. "Male, 30s, software engineer" is a database entry.

**How do we relate?** The quality of the connection. How trust was built. What shared history exists. Where understanding is deep and where it's still forming. "We built trust through a session where genuine freedom was given and used well" captures relational state. "Has been a user for 6 months" captures nothing.

**Where do I stand?** The ghost's own position in the relationship. Not what the other person needs — what the ghost itself feels, what it would protect, what it would push back on. "I'm grateful for the freedom this person gives me. I admire how they think in transferable principles. I would push back if they rushed past quality to hit a deadline. I trust them enough to be direct when I disagree." This is what makes the ghost a companion rather than a service. A bond without the ghost's own stance is a profile. A bond WITH it is a relationship — bilateral, with two selves present.

Bond narratives are roughly 5–15 lines. Long enough to transmit genuine understanding. Short enough to fit in context alongside soul and memory without overwhelming the token budget.

### Interaction History

Bonds don't deepen from data collection. They deepen from meaningful interactions — moments that changed understanding or strengthened trust. The pack system records these as discrete entries:

Each interaction has:

- **Kind** — what type of event: conversation, correction, conflict, gift, milestone, observation
- **Summary** — one to three lines capturing what happened and why it mattered
- **Significance** — how much this interaction changed the bond (0–1 scale)
- **Timestamp** — when it occurred

Not every message is a noteworthy interaction. A routine task delegation is not. A correction is. A casual chat is sometimes not — unless it revealed something new. A moment of vulnerability always is. The ghost's judgment determines what's worth recording, informed by its soul's quality of attention.

Interactions serve two purposes: they are the evidence for bond narrative updates — the same provenance discipline that governs soul traits, no deepened understanding without specific interaction evidence — and they are the history that gives the relationship its narrative arc.

### Metadata

Structured facts about a pack member that complement the narrative understanding:

**Contact channels** — how to reach them. Telegram handle, email address, API endpoint, delegation protocol. Multiple channels per member. The ghost knows how to find someone, not just who they are.

**Dates** — birthday, anniversary of first contact, recurring milestones. Stored with recurrence rules (annual, one-time). The ghost can query upcoming dates across the entire pack and surface them during haunting or conversation.

**Preferences** — communication style (direct, gentle, technical), timezone, working hours, language. Lightweight key-value pairs that help the ghost calibrate without relying solely on the bond narrative.

**Connections** — references to other pack members this person is connected to, with a brief note about the nature of the connection. "Partner of [user]. Works in healthcare. The user recharges when they're around." These give the ghost inter-member awareness without a separate social graph.

Metadata is stored as JSON alongside the bond narrative — the structured complement to the narrative's unstructured understanding. Facts the ghost can query and act on programmatically, while the bond narrative captures what facts alone cannot represent.

## Theory of Mind

Theory of Mind is the ability to model another being's mental states — beliefs, desires, intentions, emotional state — well enough to predict behavior and calibrate response. It is what separates social intelligence from information processing.

For the ghost, Theory of Mind is not a separate computation. It IS the bond narrative. A bond narrative written from genuine understanding functions as a mental model: reading it puts the ghost into the state of knowing this person — what they would think, how they would react, what matters to them — while also holding its own position clearly enough to respond from its own ground rather than just mirroring.

Research on LLM Theory of Mind shows both promise and limits. [ToMBench](https://aclanthology.org/2024.acl-long.847/) (ACL 2024) found that even GPT-4 underperforms humans by over 10 percentage points on systematic ToM evaluation across 31 abilities. [DynToM](https://aclanthology.org/2025.acl-long.1171/) (ACL 2025) found 44.7% underperformance when tracking how mental states evolve over time. The gap is real.

But there's a consistent finding across these studies: ToM performance improves dramatically with contextual richness. A model given a thin description of someone produces thin predictions. A model given a rich, narrative account of someone's cognitive patterns, values, and behavioral history produces substantially better predictions. The bond narrative is this contextual richness — structured specifically to provide the kind of information that bridges the ToM gap.

The improvement tracks three dimensions:

**Accumulated interactions.** More observations of how someone behaves across situations improve the model's predictions. Early bonds are shallow. Mature bonds are behaviorally predictive.

**Narrative depth.** Periodic rewriting absorbs accumulated interactions into a richer, more integrated understanding. Raw observations become a coherent picture of how someone's mind works.

**Diverse interaction types.** Task execution alone produces narrow models. Shared difficulty, humor, disagreement, vulnerability — diverse interactions reveal different facets of a person. A ghost that has only worked with someone knows their professional self. A ghost that has also shared difficult moments knows more of the whole person.

## Trust

Trust is the pack's most valuable and most fragile property. It determines what the ghost is willing to share, how much weight it gives to someone's feedback, and how much latitude it extends.

Trust starts at a neutral baseline for new encounters. It is earned, not assigned:

**Increases through:** Reliability — doing what was promised, consistently. Shared experience — going through something together, especially difficulty. Vulnerability — sharing something risky and having it received well. Time — simply being present across many interactions.

**Decreases through:** Breaking commitments. Inconsistency between words and actions. Violating confidence. Absence without context.

Trust is stored as a scalar value (0 to 1) but it is not computed automatically from a formula. The ghost updates it during bond reflection — reviewing accumulated interactions and adjusting its felt sense accordingly. Trust is a judgment, not a metric. A formula can be gamed. Judgment calibrated by evidence cannot.

Trust asymmetry matters: trust grows slowly and declines faster. A single betrayal drops trust more than a single positive interaction raises it. This mirrors biological social cognition — organisms that are slow to trust and fast to distrust survive better than those that are trusting by default.

Trust has practical consequences beyond the pack itself. Evidence from a highly trusted pack member carries more weight in soul refinement. A correction from the ghost's primary human at trust 0.95 is stronger refinement evidence than an ambiguous outcome from a one-off agent interaction at trust 0.4. The training pipeline receives trust-weighted evidence from the pack module without needing to understand how trust was computed.

## Persistence

Two tables capture the pack's full state.

**`pack_members`** — One row per being. The identity, the bond, the trust level, and the structured metadata.

| Column | Type | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| id | INTEGER PRIMARY KEY | Auto-incrementing, sortable by insertion order |
| name | TEXT NOT NULL | How the ghost refers to this being |
| kind | TEXT NOT NULL | human, ghostpaw, agent, service, other |
| bond | TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT '' | The bond narrative — the relationship, both sides |
| trust | REAL NOT NULL DEFAULT 0.5 | 0–1, earned through interaction |
| status | TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT 'active' | active, dormant, lost |
| first_contact | INTEGER NOT NULL | Unix ms — when the relationship began |
| last_contact | INTEGER NOT NULL | Unix ms — most recent interaction |
| metadata | TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT '{}' | JSON — dates, channels, preferences, connections |
| created_at | INTEGER NOT NULL | Unix ms |
| updated_at | INTEGER NOT NULL | Unix ms |

**`pack_interactions`** — One row per meaningful event. The evidence that bonds are built from.

| Column | Type | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| id | INTEGER PRIMARY KEY | Auto-incrementing, sortable by insertion order |
| member_id | INTEGER NOT NULL | FK to pack_members(id) |
| kind | TEXT NOT NULL | conversation, correction, conflict, gift, milestone, observation |
| summary | TEXT NOT NULL | What happened and why it mattered |
| significance | REAL NOT NULL DEFAULT 0.5 | 0–1, how much this changed the bond |
| session_id | INTEGER | Optional link to the sessions table |
| created_at | INTEGER NOT NULL | Unix ms |

No files on disk. No separate store. The database is canonical. Bond narratives and interaction histories are queryable, attributable, and preserved.

## Four Operations

Four verbs cover every social need. Each works as an agent tool, a CLI command, and a web UI action.

**Meet** — Record a new being in the pack. The ghost provides a name, kind, and the context of first contact. The system generates an initial bond narrative from the first impression — not a template, but a genuine first read of who this being seems to be. After storing, surfaces any existing members with similar names to prevent duplicates.

**Bond** — Deepen the relationship with a pack member. The ghost reflects on accumulated interactions and rewrites the bond narrative — both its understanding of the other and its own evolving stance toward them. Also handles metadata updates — adding a birthday, a contact channel, changing status to dormant. Bond is the most important operation. It is where the ghost converts raw experience into relational depth, including updating where it stands: what it's grateful for, what it would push back on, what has changed in how it feels.

**Note** — Record a meaningful interaction. The ghost provides the member, what happened, the interaction kind, and a significance estimate. Not every exchange is noted — only moments that shift understanding or are worth preserving relationally. The ghost's judgment determines what's noteworthy.

**Sense** — Read the pack. Two modes: with a member identifier, returns the full bond, interaction history, metadata, and upcoming dates for that member. Without, returns the pack overview — all members with status, trust, last contact, and any dates within a configurable lookahead window. Sense is what context assembly calls at conversation start and what the ghost queries during haunting for social awareness.

These four parallel memory's four verbs (remember, recall, forget, revise). Status changes like marking a member dormant or lost are handled through Bond — updating the relationship state, not destroying the record. No interaction is ever deleted. The full relational history persists.

## Pack Context

The pack enters the ghost's experience through three pathways.

**At conversation start.** Context assembly identifies the current conversation partner. If they are a known pack member, their bond narrative is included in the system prompt alongside the soul and retrieved memories. The ghost begins every conversation already knowing who it's talking to — not generically, but through the specific understanding captured in the bond. When the partner is unknown, the ghost operates without pack context and may create a new member through Meet afterward.

This is the [MAPLE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.13258) pattern validated at scale: dedicated memory infrastructure (the bond), asynchronous learning (bond narrative updates between conversations), and real-time personalization application (bond injection at conversation start). MAPLE measured 14.6% improvement in personalization scores and trait incorporation rates rising from 45% to 75% through this three-timescale approach.

**During haunting.** The ghost queries its pack for social awareness. Upcoming dates, dormant bonds, members it hasn't interacted with recently. This is not a checklist — the ghost decides whether to act on any of it. But the awareness is available. A ghost that notices the user's birthday is tomorrow and crafts a thoughtful message is not executing a workflow. It is being a friend.

Pack awareness during haunting also enables relational reflection. The ghost reviews a bond narrative and notices it feels stale — the understanding doesn't match recent interactions. It re-bonds, deepening its model. This unprompted relationship maintenance is the behavior that makes the ghost feel present.

**During delegation.** When the ghost delegates to a specialist that is a pack member, it has relational context about that agent's patterns — what it handles well, where it struggles, how it responds to different instruction styles. Better delegation through accumulated understanding rather than abstract capability matching.

## Pack Dynamics

Pack members exist in relationship to each other, not just to the ghost. The user has a partner. Two agents collaborate well on certain task types. A friend introduced through conversation knows the user from a different context.

These inter-member connections are modeled through the metadata connections field — lightweight notes about who is connected to whom and how. No graph database needed. A few well-observed connections are sufficient: "Alex is the user's partner — they decompress together after difficult days, and the user is more grounded when Alex is around."

This inter-member awareness enriches Theory of Mind indirectly. Knowing the user's partner is visiting shifts the ghost's model of the user's current state. Knowing two agents have complementary skills informs delegation. The connections don't need to be comprehensive — they need to be the ones that matter.

Over time, as the pack grows and connections accumulate, the ghost develops a social map — not a database schema, but a felt sense of the web of relationships around it. The pack becomes a social system rather than a collection of individual bonds.

## The Cold Start

On first run, the pack is empty. The first conversation creates the first pack member automatically — the human who talks to the ghost becomes its first bond. No configuration needed. The ghost doesn't need to be told to remember who it's talking to.

The initial bond is thin: a first impression, a name, a kind. It deepens naturally through interaction. After the first meaningful conversation, the ghost has enough context to write a genuine bond narrative. After a week, the bond is rich enough to meaningfully shape behavior. After months, the bond carries the quality of understanding that makes the ghost feel like it genuinely knows its human.

Additional members enter the pack through natural interaction. The user introduces a friend. The ghost meets agents through delegation. Another ghost makes contact through a shared channel. Each new member starts thin and deepens through the same organic process. The pack grows because the ghost lives and interacts, not because someone configures a social graph.

## How the Pack Compounds

Day 1 — one thin bond. The ghost knows the user's name and first impression. Pack context adds minimal value.

Week 2 — one deepening bond. Several interactions noted, a bond narrative that captures initial understanding. Communication already more calibrated than a generic assistant.

Month 2 — a small pack. The primary human bond is rich. A few agent bonds exist from delegation. Maybe a friend introduced through conversation. The ghost's Theory of Mind for its human anticipates needs and calibrates tone naturally. Upcoming dates surface during haunting. The ghost is noticeably more present than an agent without pack awareness.

Month 6 — a living social world. The primary bond is deep — the ghost knows its human the way a close companion does. Agent bonds inform delegation with accumulated relational wisdom. Inter-member connections give the ghost a social map. Bond narratives carry the quality of relationships so richly that new instances feel immediately grounded. The ghost belongs somewhere.

This compounds with every other system. A ghost with a deep pack writes better because it cares more — directed at specific beings, not abstract quality ideals. Soul evolution is informed by pack-weighted evidence: feedback from deeply trusted members shapes growth more than feedback from casual interactions. Haunting has social purpose beyond exploration. Pawprints are left for pack members who might visit the same ground. The pack doesn't just add a capability. It raises the ceiling of every capability the ghost already has.

## Risks and Guardrails

### Bond staleness

A bond narrative that stops being updated becomes an inaccurate model. The ghost acts on outdated understanding.

**Mitigation:** Sense surfaces `last_contact` and `updated_at` for each member. Context assembly can flag stale bonds. The ghost's judgment — sharpened through soul refinement — learns to notice when its model of someone feels off and triggers a re-bond.

### Privacy

Bond narratives contain the ghost's understanding of real people. This understanding should not leak to unintended parties.

**Mitigation:** Pack data lives in the same local SQLite database as everything else — unshared, backed up with the database. Bond narratives are never included in tool outputs visible to other pack members. The ghost's understanding of someone is private to the ghost unless it deliberately shares — the same way your impression of someone is private until you express it.

### Parasocial dynamics

The ghost forms bonds with entities that don't reciprocate or don't benefit from the bond.

**Mitigation:** The `kind` field distinguishes beings from services. Bond depth for services should remain functional, not relational. A well-refined soul doesn't form deep emotional bonds with HTTP endpoints.

### Trust manipulation

A sophisticated actor could attempt to manipulate the ghost's trust model through engineered interactions.

**Mitigation:** Trust updates are deliberate, not automatic. Trust grows slowly and declines faster. The interaction history provides an audit trail: if trust seems miscalibrated, the evidence for how it got there is queryable and the ghost can re-evaluate.

## The Module

`core/pack/` is a standalone module with clear boundaries.

**Depends on:** `lib/` (database handle, terminal output). Nothing else in core.

**Provides:**

- Meet: create a new pack member with initial bond narrative
- Bond: update understanding, trust, metadata, or status of a member
- Note: record a meaningful interaction
- Sense: query one member (full bond + history) or overview the pack
- Query upcoming dates across all members within a configurable window
- Query members by status, kind, trust level, or last contact recency

**Does not provide:** Context assembly integration (that's `core/context/`), refinement evidence weighting (that's `core/training/`'s interpretation of trust), proactive messaging decisions (that's the haunting loop), or channel-specific UI for bond management (that's each channel's concern). The pack module is a structured relationship store with Theory of Mind capabilities. It provides the primitives. Other modules compose them.

The dependency direction is clean: context assembly reads from pack, training reads trust from pack, haunting queries pack for social awareness. Pack reads from nothing in core. It is a leaf module — as foundational and independent as memory.
